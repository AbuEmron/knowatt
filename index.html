Grounding vs. bonding, finally clear.

The thing most electricians fudge — and it's on every exam. Here's the whole idea in plain language, then the picture.

Lesson 04 · Grounding & BondingApprentice → Master

Grounding connects the electrical system to the planet — the actual dirt. Bonding ties all the metal together so electricity always has a fast way home to where it came from.

The part everyone gets backwards

When something faults, people think the earth clears it. It doesn't. The dirt is too high-resistance to trip a breaker. What trips the breaker is the bond — the low-impedance metal path carrying fault current straight back to the source.

The "aha": Earth is for voltage stabilization and lightning. The bond is what keeps you alive. Mix them up on the exam and you lose points; mix them up in the field and someone gets hurt.
